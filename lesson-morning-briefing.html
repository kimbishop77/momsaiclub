Set Up Your AI Morning Briefing

What you'll build: every morning, Claude reads your calendar and email and sends you a short, friendly summary of your day — before you've even had coffee.

This is one of those setups that sounds like it requires a tech person and actually takes about five minutes. When you're done, you'll wake up to something like: "Morning! You have 3 things today: dentist for Ella at 2, the school fundraiser form is due (email from PTA last night), and your 10am call moved to 11."

What you'll need

  • The Claude app on your phone or computer (a paid plan)
  • A Gmail account and Google Calendar
  • Five quiet minutes ☕

The steps

1

Connect your Gmail and Calendar

In the Claude app, open Settings → Connectors (on some versions it's called "Extensions" or lives under your profile menu). Find Gmail and Google Calendar and click Connect on each. Google will ask you to sign in and approve — this is the standard Google permission screen, the same one you see when you sign into any app with Google.

[ Screenshot: Connectors screen with Gmail and Google Calendar connected ]

Menu names shift a little between app versions — if you don't see "Connectors," look for anything named Settings, Extensions, or Integrations. You're looking for the list of apps Claude can connect to.

2

Ask for a briefing right now — as a test

Before scheduling anything, make sure the connection works. Start a new chat and copy-paste this:

COPY THIS PROMPT Look at my calendar and my unread emails from the last 24 hours. Give me a short morning briefing: today's schedule, anything that needs a reply or action from me, and anything coming up tomorrow I should prep for. Keep it friendly and brief — like a helpful friend, not a report.

Claude will ask permission to read your email and calendar the first time. Say yes, and check that the briefing looks right.

3

Make it automatic

Now the magic part. Send this message:

COPY THIS PROMPT That was great. Please run this exact briefing for me automatically every morning at 7:00am.

Claude sets up a scheduled task, and that's it — you're done. Tomorrow at 7, your briefing arrives on its own.

[ Screenshot: Claude confirming the scheduled task ]

Make it yours

The prompt is yours to tweak — just tell Claude what to change, in plain English:

  • "Also include the weather for [your town]."
  • "Only flag emails from the school or my boss."
  • "Add one dinner idea based on how busy my evening looks." (Yes, really.)
  • "On Sundays, give me a preview of the whole week instead."
Privacy note, mom-to-mom: Connecting Gmail means Claude can read your email to summarize it — same idea as any email app on your phone. You can disconnect it anytime in the same Connectors menu, and it's your account: nobody else (including other family members' devices) gets access.

If something doesn't work

  • Claude says it can't see your email: the connector isn't connected — go back to step 1.
  • The briefing is too long / too chatty: reply "shorter, just the essentials" and ask Claude to update the scheduled task.
  • Wrong time: say "move my morning briefing to 6:30am." Done.

That's the whole lesson. You just automated the mental load of "what's happening today?" — one less thing to hold in your head. 💛
